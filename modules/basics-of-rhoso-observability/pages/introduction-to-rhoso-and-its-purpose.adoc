#  Introduction to RHOSO and its purpose

Red Hat OpenStack Services on OpenShift (RHOSO) represents a significant evolution in the deployment and management of OpenStack environments. At its core, *RHOSO stands for Red Hat OpenStack on OpenShift*. This new architectural paradigm shifts the traditional deployment model, enabling OpenStack (OSP) to run directly as a workload on top of Red Hat OpenShift Container Platform (RHOCP).

The primary purpose of RHOSO is to bring the robust capabilities of OpenStack for managing virtual machines and bare metal resources into the modern, cloud-native operational framework provided by OpenShift. This integration offers substantial advantages:

*   *Unified Management and Operations:* By deploying OpenStack services as a workload on OpenShift, administrators can leverage OpenShift's powerful orchestration, automation, and lifecycle management features across their entire infrastructure stack, from containers to virtual machines.
*   *Enhanced Observability:* The shift to an OpenShift layered architecture required a complete rearchitecture of how telemetry signals from the OpenStack environments are collected and stored. RHOSO Observability is designed to provide comprehensive insight into your RHOSO environments.

This new observability approach in RHOSO specifically aims to:

*   *Leverage OpenShift Tooling:* Integrate seamlessly with familiar and proven OpenShift Observability tools such as the Cluster Observability Operator (COO), Cluster Logging Operator (CLO), and the OpenShift Console UI. This provides a consistent monitoring experience for customers already familiar with OpenShift.
*   *Retain OpenStack Telemetry:* While embracing OpenShift tools, RHOSO Observability also maintains compatibility with native OpenStack telemetry solutions like Ceilometer and AODH (Alarming service for OpenStack). This ensures that OpenStack administrators can continue to use OpenStack-native solutions.
*   *Improve Integration with the OpenShift Ecosystem:* By building on OpenShift's foundation, RHOSO better integrates into the broader OpenShift ecosystem, enabling more streamlined workflows and tooling consolidation.
