#  Understanding the Chargeback feature in RHOSO

[[understanding-chargeback-rhoso]]
= Understanding the Chargeback Feature in RHOSO

The ability to accurately track and attribute resource consumption to specific projects, departments, or users is a cornerstone of effective cloud financial management. In the context of Red Hat OpenStack Services on OpenShift (RHOSO), this critical capability is provided by the Chargeback feature. It transforms raw telemetry data into actionable insights, enabling organizations to achieve greater cost transparency and accountability within their private cloud infrastructure.

== What is Cloud Chargeback?

At its core, "chargeback" refers to the process of allocating the cost of IT services and resources consumed back to the business units, departments, or projects that used them. In a traditional IT environment, this might involve tracking hardware, software licenses, and personnel time. In a dynamic cloud environment like RHOSO, chargeback focuses on quantifying the usage of virtual resources such as CPU, RAM, storage, network bandwidth, and specific services.

The primary goals of implementing chargeback in a cloud environment include:

*   **Cost Recovery:** Recouping the operational expenses of the cloud from the internal consumers.
*   **Cost Visibility and Transparency:** Providing clear visibility into who is consuming what resources and at what cost, fostering better resource management decisions.
*   **Resource Optimization:** Encouraging users to optimize their resource consumption when they are aware of the associated costs.
*   **Budgeting and Planning:** Supplying data for accurate future budgeting and capacity planning.

== RHOSO's Approach to Chargeback: Leveraging CloudKitty

RHOSO integrates a powerful and flexible rating service known as *CloudKitty* (often referred to as the Rating service) to deliver its chargeback capabilities. CloudKitty is an OpenStack project designed specifically to collect, process, and rate telemetry data, making it an ideal fit for the RHOSO ecosystem.

As stated in the context, CloudKitty can be enabled through the *Telemetry Operator* in RHOSO clouds. This integration allows RHOSO to:

*   **Bridge Telemetry and FinOps:** CloudKitty acts as a vital link, transforming the raw telemetry data collected from your OpenStack environment (which is managed and delivered by RHOSO) into structured, reliable data for financial operations (FinOps) middleware. This means that the days of treating your private cloud as a "free-for-all buffet" are over; you can now actively rate and account for usage.
*   **Provide Structured Data:** Whether your organizational goal is strict cost recovery from internal departments or simply providing transparent visibility into resource consumption, CloudKitty ensures you have the accurate, granular data required to make informed financial decisions.
*   **Enable Customization:** CloudKitty is a highly customizable capability within the RHOSO ecosystem, introduced in feature release 5. This allows administrators to define flexible rating rules that align with their specific business models and cost allocation policies.

[[chargeback-process-flow-overview]]
=== Overview of the Chargeback Process Flow in RHOSO

The chargeback process in RHOSO, powered by CloudKitty, generally follows these steps:

.   **Data Collection:** The Telemetry Operator, which manages services like CloudKitty, continuously collects usage metrics (e.g., CPU hours, GB of RAM, GB of storage, network egress) from various OpenStack services within the RHOSO environment.
.   **Data Ingestion and Processing:** CloudKitty ingests this raw telemetry data. It processes and normalizes the data, preparing it for rating.
.   **Rating Rule Application:** Administrators define *rating rules* (also known as rating definitions) that specify how different resource consumptions should be priced. These rules can be complex, incorporating varying rates based on resource type, project, time of day, or other custom attributes.
.   **Charge Calculation:** Based on the collected usage data and the applied rating rules, CloudKitty calculates the monetary charges for each consuming entity (e.g., a project or tenant).
.   **Reporting and Analysis:** The calculated charges are then made available through reports and invoices, allowing administrators and users to analyze consumption patterns, review costs, and ensure accountability. This data can also be integrated into external FinOps or billing systems.

[[integration-chargeback-observability]]
=== Integration of Chargeback with RHOSO Observability

Chargeback in RHOSO is intrinsically linked with the broader observability platform. Observability tools provide the foundational metrics and logs necessary for chargeback to function. Without robust metric collection, accurate charge calculations would be impossible.

However, it's crucial to understand a key nuance highlighted in the context:

*   *Observability Platform Limitations:* "The observability platform available with RHOSO does not guarantee the delivery of metrics. Metrics are exposed for scraping but they are not cached. If data is dropped there is no ability to retrospectively fill in gaps in the data, which might result in incomplete metrics."

This means that while the observability platform *exposes* metrics for consumption by services like CloudKitty, it does not inherently guarantee 100% data fidelity or provide mechanisms for historical data recovery in case of drops. This is a critical consideration for maintaining the accuracy and integrity of your chargeback reports. When planning and implementing chargeback, it's essential to:

*   **Monitor Data Flow:** Regularly monitor the health and performance of your telemetry collection and CloudKitty ingestion processes.
*   **Understand Impact of Gaps:** Be aware that any gaps in metric data could lead to under-reporting of resource consumption and, consequently, inaccurate charge calculations.
*   **Develop Reconciliation Strategies:** Consider implementing strategies to reconcile potential data discrepancies or to clearly communicate the potential for minor variations to stakeholders.

By understanding these aspects, organizations can effectively leverage RHOSO's chargeback feature to gain unprecedented control and transparency over their cloud costs.
